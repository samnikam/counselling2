Privacy Policy

This portal does not automatically capture any personal information (such as name, phone number or email address) that allows us to identify you individually, except where you voluntarily choose to provide it.

Information we collect

  • Voluntarily submitted data — name, age, gender, contact details, institution, and the details you enter while registering, booking an appointment, or completing a self-assessment.
  • Technical data — anonymous log information such as browser type, date and time of access, and pages visited, used only to measure usage and improve the site.

How the information is used

  • To schedule and deliver counselling sessions, career guidance and youth development programmes.
  • To respond to your queries and grievances.
  • To generate anonymised, aggregate statistics for programme planning. Aggregate reports never identify an individual.

Counselling confidentiality

Information shared during counselling and self-assessment is treated as confidential and is accessible only to the assigned counsellor and authorised personnel on a need-to-know basis. It is not disclosed to your institution, employer or family without your consent, except where disclosure is required by law or where there is a serious and imminent risk to the life or safety of any person.

Sharing and retention

We do not sell, trade or rent personal information. Data may be shared with other government departments or agencies only where required for service delivery or mandated by law. Personal data is retained only as long as necessary for the purpose for which it was collected, and thereafter archived or deleted as per applicable record-retention rules.

Your rights

Consistent with the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023, you may request access to, correction of, or erasure of your personal data, and may withdraw consent, by writing to the Nodal Officer at info@anantnagyouth.in. Withdrawal of consent may limit our ability to provide certain services.

Cookies

This portal uses only essential cookies required to maintain your login session. No cookies are used for advertising or cross-site tracking.

Accessibility Statement

We are committed to ensuring that this portal is accessible to all users, including persons with disabilities, in line with the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016, the Guidelines for Indian Government Websites (GIGW) and the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1.

Conformance status: this portal partially conforms to WCAG 2.1 Level AA. It has been built to the standard but has not yet completed an independent accessibility audit. A formal audit is planned, and this statement will be updated with the outcome.

Date of this statement: 21 July 2026. Last reviewed: 21 July 2026.

Measures taken so far:

  • The site uses semantic HTML structure, meaningful headings and descriptive link text.
  • All content is navigable using a keyboard alone; visible focus indicators are provided.
  • Images that convey information carry alternative text.
  • Colour contrast is designed to meet WCAG AA ratios, and colour alone is not used to convey meaning.
  • The layout is responsive and supports browser text resizing up to 200% without loss of content.

If you encounter any page that you are unable to access, please write to info@anantnagyouth.in with the page address and the difficulty faced. We will endeavour to provide the information in an accessible format within seven working days.

Right to Information

Information relating to this portal and the services delivered through it may be sought under the Right to Information Act, 2005.

  • Public Information Officer (PIO): Counselling & Youth Development Center, Office of the Deputy Commissioner, Anantnag, Jammu & Kashmir – 192101
  • First Appellate Authority: Deputy Commissioner, Anantnag
  • Email: info@anantnagyouth.in

Applications must be accompanied by the prescribed fee. A reply is ordinarily furnished within 30 days of receipt of the application, as provided under the Act.

Grievance Redressal

If you have a complaint regarding any service, content or conduct connected with this portal, you may approach the Grievance Officer designated below.

  • Grievance Officer: District Nodal Officer (Youth Affairs)
  • Address: Counselling & Youth Development Center, Office of the Deputy Commissioner, Anantnag, Jammu & Kashmir – 192101
  • Email: info@anantnagyouth.in
  • Working hours: Monday to Saturday, 10:00 AM – 5:00 PM (except gazetted holidays)

Every grievance is acknowledged within three working days and ordinarily disposed of within thirty days.

Complaints may also be lodged online on the Jammu & Kashmir Integrated Grievance Redressal and Monitoring System (JK-IGRAMS) at jkgrievance.in, which is the designated grievance platform for this Union Territory.

Help

Use the navigation bar at the top of every page to reach the main sections of the portal. To book a counselling session, open Book Appointment and complete the form; you will receive a confirmation against the contact details you provide.

This portal is best viewed in the latest versions of Chrome, Firefox, Edge or Safari with JavaScript enabled, at a minimum screen resolution of 1024×768. Documents in PDF format require a PDF reader, which is available free of charge from several vendors.

For any technical difficulty, write to info@anantnagyouth.in describing the problem and the page address.

Feedback

Your feedback helps us improve this portal and the services delivered through it. You may send comments, suggestions or corrections regarding the content, design or accessibility of this website using the form on the Contact Us page, or by writing to info@anantnagyouth.in.

Feedback is acknowledged within three working days. Please note that feedback is not a substitute for a formal complaint — if you wish to lodge a grievance regarding a service or an official, please use the Grievance Redressal route, which carries statutory timelines.
